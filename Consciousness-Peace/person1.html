What is Consciousness?

Definition

Consciousness is your awareness of yourself and the world around you—encompassing all thoughts, memories, feelings, sensations, and environmental perceptions that make up your subjective experience.

Key Points

  • Consciousness = Awareness + Wakefulness
  • It's the stream of experience that flows through your mind
  • Both subjective (internal thoughts) and objective (external world awareness)
  • Continuously shifting yet feels unified and coherent

Historical Evolution of Consciousness Studies

17th Century

René Descartes

"I think, therefore I am" - Foundation of consciousness philosophy

Late 1800s

Wilhelm Wundt

First experimental psychology lab - Scientific approach begins

Early 1900s

Freud's Model

Unconscious, preconscious, conscious - Layered understanding

1950s-60s

Cognitive Revolution

Consciousness returns to mainstream psychology

1990s

"Decade of the Brain"

Neuroimaging advances - Seeing consciousness in action

2000s

Neural Correlates

NCCs identified - Mapping consciousness to brain activity

2024-2025

Breakthrough Studies

Allen Institute study challenges existing theories
