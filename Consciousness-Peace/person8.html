Case Study 1: Split-Brain Patient

Background: Patient JW underwent corpus callosotomy severing the brain’s hemispheric connection.

Findings:

  • Self: Two separate conscious selves appear in each hemisphere.
  • Mind: Left hemisphere verbalizes; right acts independently.
  • Intelligence: Both hemispheres solve problems independently.
  • Ego: The left hemisphere creates stories to rationalize the other’s actions.

This demonstrates the independence and complexity of consciousness components.

Case Study 2: Meditation Master

Subject: Tibetan monk with 40+ years meditative practice.

Observations:

  • Self: Expanded sense beyond personal limits.
  • Mind: Extraordinary control over attention and emotions.
  • Intelligence: High meta-cognitive awareness.
  • Ego: Reduced attachment to self-concept.

Brain imaging reveals increased gamma activity and altered connectivity in relevant neural networks.

Case Study 3: Social Media Influencer

Subject: 25-year-old influencer with 1 million followers.

Challenges:

  • Self: Disconnection from authentic identity.
  • Mind: Overloaded by constant feedback and opinions.
  • Intelligence: High analytical capability, lower emotional intelligence.
  • Ego: Fragile and dependent on external validation.

Undergoes therapy focused on ego integration and self-reconnection.

Case Study 4: Recovery from Coma

Patient: 28-year-old with severe brain injury recovering consciousness.

Progress:

  • Self: Gradual return of self-awareness and continuity.
  • Mind: Restored information processing in stages.
  • Intelligence: Cognitive functions regain progressively.
  • Ego: Rebuilding identity accounting for new limitations.

New therapies based on neurotransmitter network modulation foster recovery.
