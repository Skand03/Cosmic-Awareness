Defining the Self (Unit)

The Self is the central sense of "I" within consciousness. It represents the observing and experiencing unit.

  • Provides personal identity and continuity over time
  • Observes thoughts, feelings, and sensations
  • Emerges in early development and evolves through life experiences
  • Enables self-recognition, reflection, and ownership of experiences

Neuroscience locates self-related processes mainly in the Default Mode Network (DMN), including the medial prefrontal cortex, posterior cingulate cortex, and angular gyrus.

Slide 7

Functions and Real-Life Examples

The self performs multiple functions:

  • Self-recognition: Awareness of "this is me" (e.g., mirror test in toddlers)
  • Temporal continuity: Maintains identity despite changes over time
  • Self-agency: Ownership of thoughts, choices, and actions
  • Perspective-taking: Recognizes self as separate from others
  • Self-reflection: Ability to evaluate personal thoughts and behavior

Example: Children passing the mirror test (around 18-24 months) demonstrates a developed sense of self.

Slide 8

Subcomponents of the Self

The self is not a single entity; it has interacting subcomponents:

  • Physical Self: Awareness of your body and sensory experiences
  • Mental Self: Thoughts, beliefs, and cognitive processes
  • Emotional Self: Feelings, moods, and emotional regulation
  • Social Self: Identity in relation to society and others
  • Spiritual Self: Sense of meaning, purpose, and transcendence

These subcomponents dynamically interact to create a holistic conscious self.

Slide 9

Clinical and Theoretical Significance

  • Dissociative disorders show fragmentation or disruption of self-awareness
  • Dementia and neurodegenerative diseases impair self-recognition and continuity
  • Theoretical models link the self to meta-cognition and conscious integration
  • Research explores brain networks and mechanisms underlying the emergence of the self

Understanding the self helps develop therapeutic approaches for psychological and neurological disorders.
