Slide 18

Component Interactions Overview

The four main components interact as follows:

  • Self: Provides foundation and continuity across time.
  • Mind: Processes information and experiences.
  • Intelligence: Analyzes and makes decisions.
  • Ego: Shapes identity and social behavior.

These components form a dynamic system producing conscious experience and behavior.

Slide 20

Integration Patterns

Healthy Integration:

  • Components work harmoniously
  • Flexible adaptation to life situations
  • Clear boundaries prevent confusion
  • Heightened self-awareness across components

Problematic Integration:

  • Dominance of one component
  • Rigid, inflexible behavior
  • Components working against each other
  • Lack of self-awareness and insight
Slide 21

Modern Neuroscience Findings

  • Consciousness is distinct from intelligence; one can be intelligent yet unconscious.
  • Perception is primary in consciousness rather than abstract reasoning.
  • Default Ascending Arousal Network (DAAN) plays a crucial role in awareness.
  • New therapeutic approaches target these integration networks.
  • Better understanding leads to improved treatment for disorders.
