Slide 12

Types of Intelligence in Consciousness

Intelligence manifests in multiple forms, crucial for our conscious experience:

  • Logical/Analytical: Problem-solving, critical thinking, reasoning ability.
  • Emotional Intelligence: Understanding and managing own and others’ emotions.
  • Instinctive/Limbic: Rapid, intuitive responses.
  • Intuitive Intelligence: Recognizing patterns, subconscious insights.
  • Meta-Cognitive Awareness: Awareness of one’s own thinking processes.

These types work in concert to guide our decisions, creativity, and social interactions.

Slide 13

Functions of Intelligence

Key functions include:

  • Analysis: Breaking down information into manageable parts.
  • Decision Making: Choosing among alternatives based on reasoning and intuition.
  • Pattern Recognition: Identifying and predicting trends and regularities.
  • Problem Solving: Overcoming challenges through creative and logical approaches.
  • Learning: Adjusting knowledge and behavior based on experience.

Modern research emphasizes intelligence's complexity and its separation from consciousness itself.

Slide 14

Example: Medical Diagnosis

Consider a doctor diagnosing a patient:

  • Logical Intelligence: Evaluates symptoms and clinical data systematically.
  • Emotional Intelligence: Understands patient’s feelings and anxieties.
  • Intuitive Intelligence: Picks up subtle cues and gut feelings.
  • Meta-Cognitive Awareness: Reflects on own assumptions and biases for better judgment.

Such complex interplay leads to effective, conscious decision making impacting patient outcomes.
